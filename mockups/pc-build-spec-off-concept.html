Right. 32GB keeps the timeline, previews, and export queue in memory at once without paging to disk.

Correct pick: 32GB. 8GB and 16GB choke on multi-stream editing, and 64GB is real money that doesn’t buy this ticket anything more.

Right. This build draws about 255W under load; 450W covers it with headroom to spare.

Correct pick: 450W. The 5900X and RTX 4060 together draw about 255W, so 250W comes up short. 450W is the cheapest option that actually covers it.

Right. NVMe speed matters for scrubbing large media files, and 1TB holds a project without constant offloading.

Correct pick: 1TB NVMe SSD. The SATA drives are both too slow and too small for raw 4K footage; 2TB NVMe is fine but not needed for this budget.

Right. A 120mm tower keeps a 105W chip cool through long render sessions without the AIO’s price tag.

Correct pick: 120mm tower air cooler. Stock cooling isn’t rated for sustained render loads on this chip, and the 240mm AIO is more than this budget needs.

Right. The 5700G’s integrated graphics already cover email, browsing, and spreadsheets, so this bay stays empty.

Correct pick: integrated graphics only. This desk never touches anything a video card would help with, and the 1650 doesn’t even fit this case’s 170mm limit.

Right. 8GB is plenty for a browser, a mail client, and a spreadsheet running side by side.

Correct pick: 8GB. This ticket’s workload doesn’t ask for more, and every step up just shrinks the budget for no benefit.

Right. A 65W CPU with no discrete GPU barely taxes a 250W supply.

Correct pick: 250W. Nothing in this build draws more than about 65W, so anything bigger is paying for headroom this desk will never use.

Right. 256GB of SATA SSD is more than a reception desk’s documents and browser cache will ever fill.

Correct pick: 256GB SATA SSD. NVMe speed and extra capacity don’t do anything for office documents, so they’re budget spent on nothing.

Right. The bundled cooler is rated for exactly this chip’s 65W output, so there’s nothing to upgrade.

Correct pick: stock cooler. It’s already sized for this CPU’s heat output, so any paid cooler here is money this ticket doesn’t need to spend.
