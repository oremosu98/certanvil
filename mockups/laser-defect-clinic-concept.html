Why the interval matters
Every rotating part in a laser printer has a circumference, and a flaw on it repeats at exactly that distance: drum ~76 mm on many mono lasers, fuser roller ~57 mm, transfer roller ~50 mm. Measure the repeat interval and you've identified the part before you ever open the printer.
