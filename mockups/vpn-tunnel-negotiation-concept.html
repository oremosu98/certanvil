Your company just signed a logistics partner in Frankfurt, and their ERP needs a permanent encrypted path into your London network. You own both IPSec gateways. Why three scores
A tunnel can fail three independent ways: a parameter that ducks the policy (weak cipher, PSK where certs are required), a parameter that doesn't match its peer (both sides valid, tunnel still dead), and Phase 2 subnets that don't mirror (each end's local must be the other's remote). Each is scored on its own — real negotiation failures come labelled the same way.
